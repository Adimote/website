Our goal is to inspire the next generation of engineers

What we do

We challenge teams of 16 to 18 year-olds to design, build and develop autonomous robots to compete in our annual competition. After announcing the year's game, we give teams six months to engineer their creations. We mentor teams throughout this time, as well as supply them with a kit which provides a framework they can build their robot around. Thanks to our sponsors, we provide this all for free to our teams.

The annual schedule

Kickstart

At the beginning of the academic year we have an event we call a Kickstart. Teams are given their kits and get to do some basic exercises to see how it all works. They're also introduced to the game they'll be playing at the competition.

Tech Days

Between Kickstart and the Competition we host a series of tech days in various locations throughout the UK. Usually hosted on a weekend, teams can bring their robots and get some help from volunteers.

The Competition

In the spring we host a competition for all of our participating schools. Teams bring their robots for a weekend of tinkering and matches. Successful teams advance into the knockout stages, and can go on to win prizes.
